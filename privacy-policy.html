PRIVACY POLICY

Kippy

Your privacy matters to us. Here's exactly what happens with your data.

Last updated: July 3, 2026

1Introduction

Kippy is a trip expense-splitting app that helps you track shared and personal travel spending, split costs with friends, and plan trips. This policy explains what information Kippy interacts with and how it's handled. We built Kippy to keep your data on your own device, and we take your privacy seriously — this document is meant to be straightforward, not full of legal jargon.

2Data We Collect

Kippy stores all of your data locally on your device only. There are no user accounts, no sign-ups, and no cloud sync. This means:

  • Trip details, expenses, budgets, member names, and settlement history are saved directly on your phone.
  • Nothing is uploaded to or stored on any server we operate — because we don't operate any.
  • If you switch phones or delete the app, that locally-stored data does not transfer or persist anywhere else.

We never see, collect, or have access to your trip or expense data.

3Camera & Photo Library

Kippy asks for camera and photo library access only when you choose to attach a receipt photo to an expense. These photos are:

  • Stored locally on your device, alongside the rest of your expense data.
  • Never uploaded to us or any third party.
  • Only accessed when you explicitly choose to take or select a photo — Kippy does not access your camera or photo library in the background.

4Google Places API

Kippy's "Kip's Favs" feature uses the Google Places API to suggest restaurants, attractions, and other points of interest near your trip destination. To do this, the destination name you enter (e.g. "Tokyo" or "Paris") is sent to Google as a search query. This request does not include your name, expenses, or any other personal or trip data — only the destination text needed to fetch recommendations.

This use is subject to Google's own privacy policy, available at policies.google.com/privacy.

5Unsplash API

Kippy uses the Unsplash API to automatically fetch a cover photo for a trip based on its destination name. As with Google Places, only the destination text is sent to Unsplash as a search query — no personal or trip data accompanies it.

This use is subject to Unsplash's own privacy policy, available at unsplash.com/privacy.

6Exchange Rate API

To support multi-currency trips, Kippy fetches current currency exchange rates from a public exchange rate API. This request only asks for general exchange rate data (e.g. USD conversion rates) — no personal data, trip data, or identifying information is ever sent as part of this request.

7Data Sharing

We do not sell, rent, share, or transmit your personal data to any third party. The only external services Kippy communicates with are the three listed above (Google Places, Unsplash, and the exchange rate provider), and in each case only the minimal, non-personal data described is sent — never your trip contents, expenses, member names, or any other personal information.

8Data Deletion

Because all of your data lives locally on your device and nowhere else, you're always in full control of it. To permanently delete all Kippy data, simply uninstall the app from your device. Since there are no servers or accounts, there's no separate "delete my data" request to make — your data leaves with the app.

9Children's Privacy

Kippy is not directed at, or intended for use by, children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect any information from children under 13.
